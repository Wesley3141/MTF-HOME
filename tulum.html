Tulum

Sian Ka'an Biosphere & pristine nesting beaches

Our Projects. Support Us

Tulum sits at the heart of the Riviera Maya's most ecologically significant coastline, bordered by the UNESCO-listed Sian Ka'an Biosphere Reserve to the south. This region provides nesting habitat for green, loggerhead, and hawksbill turtles along undeveloped beaches that rank among the most productive in the Caribbean. The Marine Turtle Foundation's Tulum conservatory combines rigorous scientific monitoring with Mayan community partnerships to protect turtles across the full coastal ecosystem.

Biosphere Reserve Program

Wild beach patrols & in-situ nest protection

Species in focus

Green, loggerhead & hawksbill sea turtles

People Involved

120+ rangers, researchers & community guides


Unlike developed resort areas, Tulum's conservatory emphasizes in-situ nest protection — leaving eggs where turtles lay them whenever conditions allow. Rangers patrol 22 kilometers of beach nightly during nesting season, tagging females with passive integrated transponder (PIT) tags to build a long-term database of returning mothers. This data has revealed that some green turtles in Tulum return to nest every two to three years, traveling thousands of miles from foraging grounds in the Gulf of Mexico and Caribbean.

The Sian Ka'an lagoon system connected to Tulum's coast serves as a critical juvenile turtle nursery. Our conservatory funds water quality monitoring and mangrove restoration projects that keep these sheltered waters healthy. Mangroves act as natural filtration systems, reducing sediment runoff that would otherwise smother nearshore seagrass beds where young turtles feed.

Tulum's rapid eco-tourism growth presents both opportunities and challenges. We train local tour operators in responsible wildlife viewing guidelines and operate a visitor center where travelers learn about turtle biology, Mayan cultural connections to sea turtles, and the broader marine conservation issues facing the Mesoamerican Reef — the Western Hemisphere's largest barrier reef system.
